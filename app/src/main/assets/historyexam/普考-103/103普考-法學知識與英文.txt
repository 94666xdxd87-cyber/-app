was a young boy. In 1936, when he was 19 years old, he hurt his ankle and stopped playing soccer. He decided to become
a referee and qualified as one in the same year. In 1946, he became the first referee to wear the black uniform with white
trim that later became the standard for referees.
As a soccer referee, Aston made an important contribution to the sport. He invented the card system. In modern
soccer competitions, referees give cards to players who break rules. A yellow card is a warning. A red card means that the
player is out of the game. According to Aston, the card system prevents language problems. The cards are an international
language. Every player understands what the yellow and red cards mean.
Aston said he got the idea while stopping at a traffic light. He was driving home from a 1966 World Cup match. In
that game, a German-speaking referee didn’t make his disciplinary calls clear. Some of the players could not understand
him and had confusion in mind even after the game. So Aston came up with and pushed for the idea of showing a yellow
card for an official warning and a red card for an ejection. The card system was introduced at the 1970 World Cup finals
